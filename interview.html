📚 Interview Tips

▼
  • Be authentic. Minerva values genuine self-reflection over rehearsed, polished answers.
  • Think out loud. Share your reasoning process, not just your conclusions. Interviewers want to see how you think.
  • Use specific examples. Draw from real experiences rather than speaking in generalities.
  • Stay concise. Quality over quantity. Clear, focused responses are more impactful than rambling ones.
  • Show intellectual curiosity. Ask clarifying questions and explore ideas beyond the obvious.
  • Embrace uncertainty. Saying "I don't know, but here's how I'd think about it" is perfectly valid.
  • Connect to Minerva's values. Reference active learning, global rotation, and interdisciplinary thinking when relevant.
  • Reflect on growth. Discuss what you've learned from failures and how you've changed as a result.
